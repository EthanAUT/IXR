Introduction to Extended Reality Blog


    Week 6 Week 6 lecture covered an introduction to the use of Unreal Engine for production and design of VR applications and games. We continued work within gravitysketch on the assignment one.

    Week 5 Week 5's lecture focused on designing interactive elements and environments for VR using examples of challenges unique to designing interaction in VR. These topics included the challenges relating to movement and navigation, picking up objects, and others. In this weeks lab we continued our work on gravitysketch.

    Week 4 The week four lecture covered topics relating to designing spaces for Vr and interaction using examples of immersive environments and storytelling within games and unique vr experiences. During the lab we continued work on our self portraits within gravitysketch.

    Week 3 In week three we continued to use gravity sketch, getting more familiar with the available tools and their unique quirks when working in a virtual space. This also led some students to experience one fo the major issues associated with VR, that being motion sickness, and had to step out of the headset for breaks.

    Week 2 In week two, we began to explore the software gravity sketch in preparation for the first assignment in which we would have to use the program to create a 'Self Portrait'.

    Week 1 Week One Lecture:
    The week one lecture covered the basics of augmented and virtual reality with a brief history of the topic and its associated technologies.

    Week One Lab:
    Our lab in week one allowed students to get a feel for Virtual reality through use of Vive headsets and the software tiltbrush. Using this software students were able to collaborate in groups in the creation of 3d artwork and models.
